“Nelle” Harper Lee, the author of "To kill a mockingbird" was born on April 28, 1926. She grew up in the 1930s in a rural southern Alabama town. She grew up in Monroeville, a small town in southwest Alabama. Her father was a lawyer who also served in the state legislature from 1926–1938. After she attended public school in Monroeville she attended Huntingdon College, a private school for women in Montgomery for a year and then transferred to the University of Alabama and later transferred to Oxford. She wrote essays and short stories but none of them was published until she was enouraged to finish a short story into a novel.
